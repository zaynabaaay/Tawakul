Part One — The Destination

What is Jannah?

مَثَلُ ٱلْجَنَّةِ ٱلَّتِى وُعِدَ ٱلْمُتَّقُونَ ۖ تَجْرِى مِن تَحْتِهَا ٱلْأَنْهَٰرُ ۖ أُكُلُهَا دَآئِمٌ وَظِلُّهَا

Under it rivers flow,

eternal is its fruit,

and its shade.

Surah Ar-Ra'd 13:35

No eye has seen it

أَعْدَدْتُ لِعِبَادِي الصَّالِحِينَ مَا لَا عَيْنٌ رَأَتْ وَلَا أُذُنٌ سَمِعَتْ وَلَا خَطَرَ عَلَى قَلْبِ بَشَرٍ

"I have prepared for My righteous servants what no eye has seen, no ear has heard, and what no human heart has ever imagined."

Hadith Qudsi — Sahih al-Bukhari 3244 / Sahih Muslim 2824

Faces like the moon

Their faces will shine like the full moon

Their combs will be of gold

Their sweat will smell of musk

They will never tire, never grieve, never grow old

Sahih al-Bukhari 3327 / Sahih Muslim 2834

In its shade

100years

In Paradise there is a tree so vast that a rider could travel in its shade for a hundred years — and never cross it.

Sahih al-Bukhari 3251 / Sahih Muslim 2826

And then — the veil is lifted

Allah will ask, "Do you wish for anything more?" They will say, "Have You not brightened our faces, admitted us to Paradise, and saved us from the Fire?" Then the veil is lifted — and nothing will be dearer to them than gazing upon their Lord.

Sahih Muslim 181

Part Two — The Levels

Paradise is not one place

إِنَّ فِي الْجَنَّةِ مِائَةَ دَرَجَةٍ … فَإِذَا سَأَلْتُمُ اللَّهَ فَاسْأَلُوهُ الْفِرْدَوْسَ

"In Paradise there are one hundred levels — between each, a distance like that between the heavens and the earth. Al-Firdaws is its highest part. Above it is the Throne of the Most Merciful, and from it spring the rivers of Paradise. So when you ask Allah — ask Him for Al-Firdaws."

Sahih al-Bukhari 2790

Eight gates, eight paths in

Bab as-Salah — prayer

Bab as-Sadaqah — charity

Bab ar-Rayyan — fasting

Bab al-Jihad — struggle

…and four more

"Whoever was among the people of prayer is called from the gate of prayer; whoever gave charity, from the gate of charity…"

Sahih al-Bukhari 3666 / Sahih Muslim 1027

Part Three — The Price

But did you think it would be free?

أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ أَن تَدْخُلُوا۟ ٱلْجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَأْتِكُم مَّثَلُ ٱلَّذِينَ خَلَوْا۟ مِن قَبْلِكُم

"Do you think you will enter Paradise without being tested like those before you? They were struck by suffering and adversity and so shaken that even the Messenger and the believers cried out: 'When will Allah's help come?' Indeed, Allah's help is always near."

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:214

Two paths, two veils

حُفَّتِ الْجَنَّةُ بِالْمَكَارِهِ وَحُفَّتِ النَّارُ بِالشَّهَوَاتِ

"Paradise is surrounded by hardships. Hellfire is surrounded by desires."

Sahih Muslim 2822 / Sahih al-Bukhari 6487

Even Jibreel feared

When Allah created Paradise, He sent Jibreel to see it. He returned: "By Your Glory — no one will hear of it but he will enter it."

Then Allah surrounded it with hardships. Jibreel looked again: "By Your Glory — I fear no one will enter it."

Then He surrounded the Fire with desires. Jibreel said: "By Your Might — I fear none will be saved from it."

Jami at-Tirmidhi 2560 / Sunan an-Nasa'i 3763

Allah's merchandise

أَلَا إِنَّ سِلْعَةَ اللَّهِ غَالِيَةٌ ۖ أَلَا إِنَّ سِلْعَةَ اللَّهِ الْجَنَّةُ

"Truly, the merchandise of Allah is precious. Truly, Allah's merchandise is Jannah."

Jami at-Tirmidhi 2450

Part Four — The Proof

Surah Al-Buruj

قُتِلَ أَصْحَٰبُ ٱلْأُخْدُودِ

Condemned are the makers of the ditch —

the fire pit, filled with fuel —

when they sat around it, watching what they did to the believers.

They resented them for no reason except that they believed in Allah.

Surah Al-Buruj 85:4–8

The story behind the verses

A king, a magician, a boy sent to learn magic — who instead found a monk, and found faith.

His faith gave him power to heal; the king tried to kill him and failed every time.

The boy said: "You will never kill me until you gather the people and say: 'In the name of Allah, the Lord of this boy.'" The king did — the boy died — and the people declared: "We believe in the Lord of this boy."

Enraged, the king dug ditches and lit fires. Believers were ordered: renounce your faith or burn. They chose the fire.

A mother hesitated at the edge, her infant in her arms — until the child spoke:
"Be patient, mother. You are upon the truth."

Sahih Muslim 3005

They did not turn back

Khabbab ibn al-Art said: a believer before us would be placed in a pit, a saw set on his head and split in two — and it would not turn him from his faith. His flesh would be combed from his bones with iron — and still he would not turn back. The Prophet ﷺ said: "By Allah, this religion will prevail… but you are being hasty."

Sahih al-Bukhari 6943

A woman of Paradise

Ibn Abbas said: "Shall I show you a woman of the people of Paradise?"

She came to the Prophet ﷺ: "I suffer seizures and become uncovered. Pray to Allah to cure me."

He said: "If you wish, be patient — and you will have Paradise. Or if you wish, I will ask Allah to cure you."

She said: "I will be patient. But pray that I do not become uncovered."

Sahih al-Bukhari 5652 / Sahih Muslim 2576

Part Five — The Path

Guarantee six — and Paradise is guaranteed for you

  1. Be truthful when you speak
  2. Keep your promises
  3. Fulfill what you're entrusted with
  4. Guard your chastity
  5. Lower your gaze
  6. Restrain your hands from harming others

"Guarantee these six for me, and I will guarantee Paradise for you."

Musnad Ahmad 22251 ('Ubadah ibn al-Samit)
