The Meaning

What Is Tawakkul?

Root: و ك ل
To entrust a matter to someone capable of taking care of it.
Al-Wakīl (الوكيل)
The Perfect Trustee and Disposer of Affairs.
Tawakkul
Entrusting your affairs to Allah while relying upon Him completely.
Setting the Scene · 622 CE

The Beginning of the Hijrah

Allah granted the Prophet ﷺ permission to migrate to Madinah.
The leaders of Quraysh decided to assassinate him.
Armed men surrounded his home and waited for morning.
Leaving Makkah

The Escape

Ali ibn Abi Talib slept in the Prophet's ﷺ bed.
The Prophet ﷺ left during the night and went to Abu Bakr's home.
Instead of traveling north toward Madinah, they traveled south.
Three Days in Hiding

The Cave of Thawr

They planned to travel south to the Cave of Thawr.
Search parties were looking for them throughout the area.
They remained hidden there for three days.
Taking the Means

Taking the Means

Asma bint Abi Bakr brought food.
Abdullah ibn Abi Bakr brought news from Makkah.
Amir ibn Fuhayrah covered tracks with his flock.
A skilled guide was arranged for the next stage of the journey.
Inside the Cave of Thawr
Abu Bakr said:

"If one of them were to look down at his feet, he would see us."

The Prophet ﷺ replied:

"What do you think of two, the third of whom is Allah?"

Sahih al-Bukhari, Sahih Muslim
Confirmed in the Qur'an

"Do not grieve; indeed Allah is with us."

Surah At-Tawbah · 9:40
A Detail Often Told

A Commonly Told Detail

A spider quickly spun a web across the entrance of the cave.
Two doves nested nearby and laid eggs.
When the searchers arrived, they assumed no one could be inside and moved on.
On the Road to Madinah

Suraqa's Pursuit

Suraqa ibn Malik pursued them hoping to claim the reward.
Each time he approached, his horse stumbled and sank into the ground.
He realized Allah was protecting the Prophet ﷺ.
He abandoned the chase and turned back.
What the Story Teaches

What the Hijrah Teaches About Tawakkul

The Prophet ﷺ took the means.
Trusting Allah does not guarantee an easy path.
The outcome was left to Allah.
A Hadith to Remember

"If you were to rely upon Allah with the reliance He deserves, He would provide for you as He provides for the birds."

Source: Jami' at-Tirmidhi
They leave hungry in the morning.
They return full in the evening.
The bird trusts Allah — but it still leaves the nest.
Putting It Into Practice

Applying Tawakkul Today

Take the means.
Make du'ā and seek guidance.
Trust Allah with the outcome.
Avoid two extremes.
