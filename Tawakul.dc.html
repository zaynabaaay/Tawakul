The Meaning

What Is Tawakul?

و-ك-ل
Linguistically
To entrust an affair to another, and to rely upon them.
✦
Islamically
Placing complete trust in Allah's plan and decree.
!
Crucially
Paired with taking the means — never passive waiting.
Setting the Scene · 622 CE

The Hijrah

◆Commanded to migrate from Makkah to Madinah after years of persecution.
◆The Quraysh placed a bounty on the lives of the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr.
◆Real precautions: hiding at the cave of Thawr, a skilled guide, Asma bringing food and news.
They planned carefully — then trusted Allah with the outcome.
"…And when you have decided, then rely upon Allah." · Qur'an 3:159
Inside the Cave of Thawr

The Moment of Fear

The pursuers came so close their feet were visible just outside the entrance.

Abu Bakr feared for the Prophet ﷺ: "If one of them looks down at his feet, he will see us."

A natural, human fear — felt even alongside the Prophet ﷺ himself.

The Prophet's Reply ﷺ
إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَنَا

"What do you think of two, the third of whom is Allah?"

Tawakul is not the absence of fear — it is trusting Allah in spite of it.
Sahih al-Bukhari · Sahih Muslim 2381 · Jami' at-Tirmidhi
Confirmed in the Qur'an
إِذْ يَقُولُ لِصَاحِبِهِ لَا تَحْزَنْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَنَا

"…when he said to his companion, 'Do not grieve; indeed Allah is with us.'"

Surah At-Tawbah · 9:40
Tranquility sent down
Sakinah upon the heart in the face of danger.
Supported by the unseen
Aided with forces they could not see.

Handling the Sources

A Note on Authenticity

Often Retold — Not Established
  • A spider's web spun across the entrance
  • Two doves nesting at the cave mouth
  • The snake-bite in the cave
What the Scholars Say
  • No authentic chain for the web or doves
  • The snake-bite report rests on a broken chain
  • The lesson stands on the Qur'an and sound hadith
Noted by Al-Albani and Ibn al-'Uthaymeen.

Journey's End

Safe Arrival in Madinah

◆Arrived safely, founded the mosque at Quba, then entered Madinah.
◆The mosque rose on land gifted by two orphan boys.
◆The Prophet ﷺ carried the bricks himself, alongside the companions.
"And whoever relies upon Allah — then He is sufficient for him."
Qur'an 65:3 — their trust through the journey was met here.
The Heart of It

Tawakul Is Not Passivity

"Tie your camel — then trust in Allah."
A guide
Expert knowledge, hired with care.
An unexpected route
South first — away from the search.
Planned supplies
Provisions carried through the nights.
A Hadith to Remember

The Hadith of the Bird

"He would provide for you as He provides for the birds: they go out hungry in the morning and return full in the evening."

Yet the bird still goes out and searches — it does not sit waiting in the nest.

Jami' at-Tirmidhi
What the Story Teaches

Lessons on Tawakul

Trust doesn't remove fear
It changes how you respond to it.
Help comes in ordinary forms
Often unexpected — a guide, a sinking horse, a quiet provision.
The outcome belongs to Allah
It is not a verdict on whether you did the right thing.
Qur'an 8:2 — trust named as a defining quality of the believers.
Putting It to Work

Practical Application

Pair dua with effort
Job search, health, big decisions — ask, then act.
Accept the outcome
Even when it isn't what you hoped for.
Keep the balance
Hold both effort and trust together.
Avoid the two extremes:
relying only on your own effort
·
using "trust" as an excuse not to act
In Summary

Recap
